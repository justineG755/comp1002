About the Polaroid Instant Camera 600

The Polaroid Instant Camera is made to give you real photos in minutes. Instead of waiting for film labs or dealing with darkrooms, this camera lets you take a picture and watch it develop right in front of you.

How it Works

Inside every film pack is a special layer of chemicals between the image surface and the backing that starts developing as soon as the camera ejects the photo. As the photo is coming out it passes between rollers that burst pods of reagent, spreading the developing chemicals evenly across the image area. The image will then slowly appears over the next few minutes.No extra steps, chemicals, or special tools needed.

The film is designed to protect itself from light while developing, which is why the image slowly fades in over time. The camera’s automatic exposure using light sensors and built-in flash helps it adjust to different lighting so you can get a clear picture with just one click.

Key Features

  • Instant development ~ Photo develops on it's own within minutes.
  • Easy Use Design ~ Simple controls just point and shoot, beginner friendly.
  • Automatic exposure ~ Balances the light for clearer pictures in different settings.
  • Built-in flash ~ Works for all shots, indoors or outdoors.
  • Self-powered film pack ~ Battery inside each pack, the camera has no internal battery.
  • Fixed Focus Lens ~ Made to capture photos without needing to adjust focus.
  • Durable Design ~ Made for everyday use and perfect to take on the go.

Different Models in the 600 Series

The Polaroid 600 series includes many different models that were released over the years. While each one has a slightly different design, they all work the same way and use the same instant 600 film.
Some popular models include...

  • Polaroid OneStep 600 ~ simple point-and-shoot design.
  • Polaroid Sun 660 ~ features Polaroid’s sonar auto-focus system.
  • Polaroid 636 CloseUp ~ adds a close-up lens for detailed shots.
  • Polaroid Impulse 600 ~ a more modern, curved body style.



The History

The first Polaroid instant cameras was introduced in 1948, changing how people took photos forever. Instead of waiting days for film processing, Polaroid made it possible to see a picture almost instantly. By 1981, the Polaroid 600 series became extremely popular for their quick developing film, easy design, and fun instant prints people could share right away. Each film pack produces 8 photos in the classic square format with the signature white border, powered by a built-in battery within the cartridge.



Why People Love it

The Polaroid Instant Camera is simple, fun, and perfect for capturing everyday moments. People love it because they can take a picture and actually physically hold it in their hands a few minutes later. They also can watch, as the image slowly appear from a blank white square which is a magical experience. It’s great for birthdays, events, vacations, and any moment you want to remember. The camera does most of the work for you, making it easy for anyone to get a good picture.
